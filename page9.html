GRADUATE EDUCATION

Masters

A masters is the first level of graduate coursework and can be obtained after you receive a bachelor’s degree. Earning a masters usually requires two years of full-time study, which amounts to 36 to 54 semester credits.

Those who pursue education as a career will undergo courses that are designed to train future educators. Fortunately, there is a wide variety of courses designed for students who wish to supplement any teaching credentials. These courses are offered at various institutions across the globe.

China is seeking to promote the development of first-class universities. China's economy is already the world's second largest and is still growing. Experiencing its culture and understanding its market growth will boost your career.

Chinese Universities provide courses taught by English for international students who enroll under self sponsorship and scholarships 

Basic Education

Basic education in China includes Pre-school education (usually three years), primary education (six years, usually starting at the age of six) and secondary education (six years).

Secondary education has two routes: academic secondary education and specialized/vocational/technical secondary education. Academic secondary education consists of junior (three years) and senior middle schools (three years). Junior middle school graduates wishing to continue their education take a locally administered entrance exam, on the basis of which they will have the option of continuing in an academic senior middle school, or entering a vocational middle school (or leave school at this point) to receive two to four years of training. Senior middle school graduates wishing to go to universities must take National Higher Education Entrance Exam (Gao Kao). According to the Chinese Ministry of Education, in June 2015, 9.42 million students took the exam.

Higher Education

Higher education is further divided into two categories: 1) universities that offer four-year or five-year undergraduate degrees to award academic degree qualifications; and 2) colleges that offer three-year diploma or certificate courses on both academic and vocational subjects. Postgraduate and doctoral programs are only offered at universities.

Adult Education

The adult education ranges from primary education to higher education. For example, adult primary education includes Workers’ Primary Schools, Peasants’ Primary Schools in an effort to raise literacy level in remote areas; adult secondary education includes specialized secondary schools for adults; and adult higher education includes traditional radio/TV universities (now online), most of which offer certificates/diplomas but a few offer regular undergraduate degrees.


MIDDLE SCHOOL EDUCATION 

Structure of the Chinese education system  

In China, the education is divided into three categories: basic education, higher education, and adult education. By law, each child must have nine years of compulsory education from primary school (six years) to junior secondary education (three years)

TIPS FOR FLYING

Here are a few tips to help take the stress out of your airport and flight experience.

If you are bringing a mobile phone and/or laptop with you, it’s a good idea to have these fully charged before you leave. You will be able to use these at the airport, but once you’re on the plane they will have to be switched off during take off and landing and in the case of your mobile, possibly the duration of the flight, depending on your airline’s regulations.

Make sure that your luggage is packed in accordance with the regulations for checked luggage and hand luggage.

Make sure you get to the airport early, as you may have to queue at check-in. Contact your airline to find out what time their check-in closes. Also allow plenty of time to find your way around.

Make sure that you keep your passport, visa(s) and air ticket in an easily accessible place, for inspection at check-in.

Once you have checked in, your air ticket will be exchanged for a boarding pass, which you will need to have available for inspection at security check-points and Passport Control.

Take note of our tips on going through Airport Security to help you get through smoothly and quickly.

Once you have gone through Airport Security, check one of the many screens around the terminal building to ensure that your flight is on time. You could also sign up for SMS or email flight status alerts. Check the boarding gate you need to go to, and at what time. Allow plenty of time to get to your boarding gate - some of them are further away than others. Boarding will be announced at least 45 minutes before departure and boarding gates will close 15 minutes before departure.

If you still have plenty of time you’ll be able to browse our Duty Free shops, have a cup of coffee and a meal and buy a book or magazine to read on the flight.

During the flight, try to drink plenty of water to combat dehydration.

Stretch your legs and try to take regular walks throughout the flight to keep the blood circulating in your legs. This will ease the discomfort of sitting for a long time.

How to Apply  

1.You may submit the application to the consular office of the Chinese Embassies or Consulates in person.  

2. Or you can entrust someone else (e.g. friends, relatives), a travel agency or a visa agency to act on your behalf if you can't make it due to valid personal reasons. Power of attorney is not required.

3. Mailed applications are not accepted and will be refused by most of Chinese embassies or consulates.

4. Appointment is not required by most embassies and consulates. But if you are required to submit application to CVASC ( Chinese Visa Application Service Centre), you may need to make an online appointment via CVASC's website.

  Processing Duration

The regular processing time is 4 working days.However in most countries, the Chinese embassies are opened to public 

only Mondays and Wednesdays. If you want to collect your visa earlier, the express service and rush service are available. Express Service: 2-3 working days processing; extra US$ 20 will be charged. Rush Service (for emergencies): 1 working day processing; extra US$ 30 will be charged. 

Visa

Student Visa is divided into X1 and X2. X1 is issued to aliens who come to China for study, advanced studies, or fieldwork for more than 6 months. X2 is issued to aliens who come to China with same purpose but for a period of less than 6 months.

The organization must be accredited for offering courses or internships to foreigners.

They will send you a JW201 or JW202 form and an admission letter. Submit these with photocopies with your application.  

You are not permitted to work on the X type visa. But part-time work and internships off campus may be authorized in near future. You should contact your university or employer after you have settled in to see what can be arranged.

For foreign students who work in violation of the regulations on the administration of foreign students working to support their study in China and work beyond the prescribed scope of jobs or prescribed time limit, they shall be deemed unlawful employment and will probably have to stop or be expelled.

X1 holders shall apply for a Temporary Residence Permit from the local Public Security Bureau (PSB) within 30 days upon entry into China.

The temporary residence permit can be issued with validity of 180 days to 5 years. X2 holders shall stay in China within the duration period as indicated on the visa, and it is not necessary for them to apply for the temporary residence permit.

Requirements

1.Applicant's original valid passport  up to at least six months with at least one blank page.

2. One accurately and truthfully completed Application Form.

3. Please affix one recent passport style color photo with full face, front view, no hat, and against a plain light background on the application form.

4. Original and a copy of approved Foreign Student Visa Application Form JW201 or JW202 (for X1 application only).

5. Original and a copy of Admission Notice from the university or Chinese school.

Note: for foreigners who want to apply for Chinese Medical Internship, the original and a copy of Application Form for Medical Internship and Admission Notice for training in practice issued by the university are required.

 Do Chinese Universities offer                      courses taught in English ?

Yes. There are bachelor, master and doctorate programs taught in English at China's universities.  Can I apply to Chinese                                      Universities directly?

You are not advised to apply directly to the university. To apply directly to universities, you are on your own. With us, however, you have a professional team to help you and fight for you – with low cost. When applying directly to an overseas university, the process can be complicated and confusing, not to mention the time-zone difference and the language barrier.

Searching information is difficult. Nearly 1000 universities in China can recruit international students. Therefore, it will be difficult for you to search school and program information if you are applying for more universities.

The procedure is complicated. Most universities do not provide online application and it’s complicated to complete application. You need to download the application forms, transfer application fee from the bank, and deliver application documents. And student cannot effectively track application status online.

School teachers cannot provide high-quality and timely service. University admission committee is processing thousands of applications every week. Accommodation cannot be guaranteed. Normally, supervisor of dorms can only speak Chinese. It will be difficult for you to book accommodation if your Chinese is not good.

Admission package might get lost during delivery. Normally, the university mail students’ admission package by air mail. It’s slow and easy to get lost. If this happens, you will miss the chance for studying in China.

No late service. Most schools don’t provide documents to guide students how to get to the university from the airport, how to complete registration and how to pay their tuition and other fees.
